Complete AdSense Policy Guide - Avoid Account Bans

Published: 2026-02-19 | Read time: 10 minutes | Category: Policy Guide

AdSense is one of the strictest advertising platforms. Policy violations can lead to permanent account bans and loss of all revenue. This article will detail all AdSense policies to help you comply with rules and ensure long-term stable income.

Why Are AdSense Policies So Strict?

AdSense's goal is to protect advertisers and user experience. Google must ensure ads are displayed on:

  • High-quality websites
  • Legitimate, compliant content
  • Genuine traffic and clicks

Policy violations not only affect your revenue but also damage the trust of the entire advertising ecosystem.

Core Policy Overview

AdSense policies are divided into 4 main categories:

  1. Content Policy - What content can be published on the website
  2. Ad Display Policy - How ads are displayed on the website
  3. Traffic Policy - Quality and sources of website traffic
  4. Prohibited Behaviors - Things that absolutely cannot be done

2. Ad Display Policy

Ad Placement Requirements

‚úÖ Recommended Ad Placements

  • Article Top - Below the title, above the fold
  • Article Middle - Interspersed between paragraphs
  • Sidebar Top - Near the navigation area
  • Footer - Non-main content area

‚ùå Prohibited Ad Placements

  • Blocking content (floating ads)
  • Misleading clicks (confusing with buttons)
  • Pop-up ads
  • Forced clicks (must click to close)
  • In-content (disrupting reading experience)

Ad Quantity Limits

Google doesn't strictly limit ad quantity, but recommends:

  • Content First: Ads should not exceed 50% of content
  • User Experience: Avoid excessive ads
  • Balance: Usually 3-5 ads are sufficient

Ad Styles

  • Use responsive ads (auto-adjust size)
  • Avoid colors that confuse with content
  • Use native ads (blend with content)
  • Don't modify ad code

3. Traffic Policy

Traffic Quality Requirements

‚úÖ High-Quality Traffic Sources

  • Search engines (Google, Bing, etc.)
  • Direct access (entering URL)
  • Social media (genuine user sharing)
  • Backlinks (citations from other websites)

‚ùå Low-Quality Traffic Sources

  • Paid-to-click (PTC) websites
  • Traffic exchange websites
  • Bot traffic, crawlers
  • Auto-refresh scripts
  • Traffic manipulation software

Click-Through Rate (CTR) Anomalies

Normal CTR range is 0.5% - 5%. If it exceeds 10%, it will trigger a review:

  • Check for fake clicks
  • Review ad placement for misleading
  • Check traffic source quality

4. Prohibited Behaviors

Click Fraud (Most Serious Violation)

‚ùå These Behaviors Will Lead to Permanent Bans

  • Clicking your own ads
  • Encouraging others to click ads
  • Paying people to click ads
  • Using software for automatic clicking
  • Obtaining clicks through deception

Other Prohibited Behaviors

  • Modifying Ad Code: Any form of code modification
  • Hiding Ads: Using CSS to hide ads
  • Blocking Ads: Other elements covering ads
  • Duplicate Submission: Applying for multiple accounts simultaneously
  • Data Manipulation: Fake traffic, fake clicks

Account Review Process

First Application Review

  1. Submit application (1-2 day review)
  2. After approval, add ad code
  3. Second stage review (1-2 weeks)
  4. Full activation (start earning)

Regular Reviews

Even after account approval, regular reviews occur:

  • Content Review: Check if new content complies with policies
  • Traffic Review: Check traffic quality and sources
  • Behavior Review: Check for policy violations

Common Questions

Q: What should I do if my account is suspended?

A: First, pause all ads, then:

  1. Check violation notifications in AdSense dashboard
  2. Locate and fix the issue
  3. Submit appeal (with detailed explanation)
  4. Wait 1-2 weeks for review

Q: How to avoid false positives?

A: Take preventive measures:

  • Regularly check AdSense reports
  • Monitor unusual traffic and clicks
  • Use Google Analytics to track traffic sources
  • Delete suspicious traffic sources
  • Maintain content quality

Q: How long should I wait to reapply after rejection?

A: Recommend waiting 1-2 months, while also:

  • Adding high-quality content (at least 10-15 articles)
  • Improving website design and user experience
  • Acquiring organic traffic
  • Ensuring full policy compliance

Q: Can I appeal a permanent ban?

A: Yes, but success rate is very low. Only when:

  • It's definitely a Google false positive (very rare)
  • You can provide sufficient evidence proving innocence

Recommendation: Prevention is better than cure. Strictly comply with policies.
